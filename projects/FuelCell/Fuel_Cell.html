 Project description:

The goal of this project is an Ultra-light, hydrogen fuel cell, single person vehicle that will travel forty miles in one hour without refueling, using six liters of compressed hydrogen. The project will cover the research design, construction and demonstration of the vehicle in a one-hour exhibition.

Qualifications and capabilities of project team - This project will be completed by the students of the Los Altos Academy of Engineering, with support from the Shatz Energy Research Center, A/C Propulsion, Cal Poly Pomona College of Engineering, and So Cal. Edison , Electric Transportation div. Irwindale Speedway.  Los Altos has a rich history of designing and constructing successful alternative energy vehicles.



Summarized work program - Student teams will manage and work on all aspects of the project. A lightweight, aerodynamic body will be designed and constructed. An efficient drive system and supplemental battery will be engineered. A lightweight, Hydrogen storage system will be integrated into the vehicle. Students and advisors from the mechanical team will spend a week at the Shatz Energy Research Center, learning about fuel cells and constructing the fuel cell stack.

Potential air quality benefits - This demonstration vehicle will be a proof of concept of a zero emission, city vehicle.

Project time schedule– this vehicle will begin testing in the spring of 2004.  Concept specifications and design for this project has already began. Procedures for handling and storage of Hydrogen will be in phase one.  The advisory group comprised of staff from the Shatz Energy Research Center, A/C Propulsion, Cal Poly Pomona College of Engineering, and So Cal. Edison, Electric Transportation div Fall 2002 aerodynamics testing and body mold fabrication.
