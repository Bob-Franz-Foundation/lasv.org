Elementary School Project
Simple Circuit Board

List of Materials
12'' x 12'' wooden board
1.5 volt AA batteries (2)
AA battery holders (2) 270-401A
drill/screwdriver
wood screws (18)
¾ amp fuses (2) 270-1062
fuse holders (2)
mini flashlight lamps (4) 272-1124
mini lamp bases (4) 272-356
solder
soldering iron
terminal blocks (2)
18 gauge wire, red and black (about 3 feet of each) red for positive, black for negative
18 gauge ring connectors (2)
wire stripper
knife switch 275-1537
zip ties (4)

Procedures for Assembly

1.Lay out all the components on the wooden board.

2.Screw the knife switch onto the board.

3.Strip two 4'' wires entirely free of their insulators.

4.Connect a wire free of insulator to one side of a terminal block so that it connects the 4 screws together. Snip off extra wire. Repeat this step with the other wire and terminal block.

5.Drill four holes for the first terminal block. Zip tie the terminal block onto the board with the side of the wiring facing out.

6.Position the lamp bases.

7.Drill four holes into the board onto the other side of where the lamp bases will be. Secure the second terminal block onto the board as step #5 with the side of the wiring facing out.

8.Screw down the two battery holders in series

9.Screw down the two fuse holders.
10.Measure the length between each component and cut wires to the appropriate length.

11.Strip both ends of a red wire (to the appropriate length that will connect from the knife switch to the first terminal block). Crimp a ring connector onto one end. Screw this end to the knife switch. Connect the other end of this wire to one of the screws on the side of the terminal block with the wiring.

12.Strip both ends of four red wires (to the appropriate length that will connect from the screws of the first terminal block to each of the lamp bases). Solder each red wire to the ring of each lamp bases.

13.Strip both ends of four black wires (to the appropriate length that will connect from the other ends of the lamp bases to the screws of the second terminal block). Solder each black wire to the other ring of each lamp base.

14.Screw lamp bases onto board.

15.Connect the ends of each red wire to each of the screws on the non-connected side of the terminal block.

16.Connect the ends of each black wire to each of the screws on the non-connected side of the second terminal block.

17.Attach the negative (black) wire of the first battery holder to one of the screws on the side of the second terminal block with the wiring.

18.Connect the battery holders in series by soldering the red wire of the first holder to the black wire of the second holder.

19.Connect the second battery holder to a fuse holder by soldering a red wire onto one side of the fuse holder.

20.Connect the two fuse holders together by soldering a red wire between the two holders.

21.Strip both ends of a red wire (to the appropriate length that will connect from the fuse holder to the knife switch). Crimp a ring connector onto one end. Solder the other end to the fuse holder. Screw the ring connector to the side of the knife switch that has the same pole as the connector to the first terminal block.

22.Put batteries and fuses into place.
