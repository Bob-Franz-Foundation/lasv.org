Introduction

Botball is a Robotic Competition held by KISS Institute of Practical Robotics . The main goal of this program is to introduce student of different levels to Engineering, Programming and Robotics.

In this competition, the students have to build an autonomous robot to archieve a certain goal, e.g. stack cups, grab foam balls and etc. In order to do this, they will have to design their own robot, construct the robot out of a Lego kit such as Mindstorms, and program the robot in C language.

During the construction and programming of the robot, students will learn different stuff such as team work, logical thinking, programming, mechnical engineering, gear system and etc. In Botball, rather the students are desired to learn, dedicate to robotics, still unsure, or new to it, they can make friends, have fun, relax in it and still learn a lot of things out of it.

To further understand more about Botball, we have interviewed some of the Los Alots Academy of Engineering Botball members about what they think about Botball:

Aaron Norris : It's what I've always wanted to do and never really considered anything else. I enjoy the competition and the building.

Cliff Fung : Botball is a place to learn, make friends, have fun and relax.

James Liu : Botball is a way to start off for what you want in life. It is a way to learn to work with other people.

Aaron Mayeda : It's a place to fully develop the brain. You have to be creative. The team was divided into builders and programmers. However, it was essential to know both sides of the program.
