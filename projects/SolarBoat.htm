Solar Boat

During the spring of the year 2004, the Los Altos Solar Boat team made their maiden voyage. This new subset of the Los Altos Academy of Engineering had worked long and hard in order to compete with their boat, Corsair , at the Solar Cup, a competition hosted by the Metropolitan Water District of Southern California at Lake Skinner. At this competition, the Los Altos Solar Boat team placed third in the rookie division and sixth overall-not bad, considering this was the first time they had they had for their first year.

The team consisted of Ann Chong, Jeffrey Cui, Jason Dao, Diego Hinojosa, Eric Huang, Eugene Kim, Chris Liu, Desiree Magat, Edwin Mejia, Robert Navarro, Michael Sanora, David Tseng, Peter Xu, Sophia Yang, and Jiayi Zhou. These students ranged from 9 th to 11 th grade, with the majority being 10 th graders.

The team was divided into several categories. The design team drafted the structure of the boat, drawing all components of the boat, excepting the steering system. The team also designed the logo. The mechanical team designed and prepared the drive train and steering of the boat. The composites team sanded the boat for less resistance, coated the surface with epoxy, and painted the boat for aesthetical purposes. The electrical team completed the electrical system, charged and discharged batteries, and made new wires. The public relations team managed finances, took notes during team meetings, set deadlines, and contacted sponsors through the phone, letters, or Internet.

There were times when it seemed as if the team would never make it to competition. Missed deadlines, procrastination, lack of communication, and tension within the team made their goal of competition a faraway dream. However, all the members pulled through and made it to Lake Skinner on time.

With the experience gained by their first attempt at the Solar Cup, the Los Altos Solar Boat team plans to build upon this prior knowledge in order to win first place this year. 

Written by Sophia Yang
Edited by Ann Chong
