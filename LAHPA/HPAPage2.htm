Los Altos Human Powered Airplane Prospectus

Project Name:

Grasshopper

 
History:

The Los Altos Human Powered Airplane project (LAHPA) started after several accomplishments in various competitions. Among these were the Solar Bikerayce 1998 in Topeka, Kansas, 1996 World Solar Challenge in Australia, and the 1995 and 1997 Winston Solar Challenge in Dallas, and the 1996 and 1997 Chrysler “Build Your Dream Vehicle” event. The idea of making the human powered airplane originated with several students. These students felt that after building several successful projects, such as two electric cars (Speed Racer 1993) and (Lightspeed 1998) and two solar cars, (Cool Runnings 1994) and (Solar Shadow 1995), the organization should advance to a new level and accomplish what others only dreamed.
The name of the HPA, Grasshopper, was decided among the members of the team. It symbolizes the desire of getting in the air. The LAHPA project was finally initiated during February, 1997.

 
Team Members:

Matt Casalicchio, Tony Chang, Joyce Chen, Theresa Chen, Aileen Chi, Nancy Chiu, Dan Frederickson, Jeffery Hsiung, Calvin Kuo, Frank Kuo, Jesse Lee, Nelson Lee, Cathay Liu, Jenny Liu, Keith Miyake, Todd Sadamoto, Edward Song, Dave Tsai

 
Description:

Grasshopper is a lightweight human powered airplane construced primarily of foam, aluminum, and mylar. It will have an approximate weight of 90 pounds.

 
Research:

The book The Gossamer Odyssey by Morton Grosser. Several sites concerning American and Japanese HPA through the World Wide Web was also read and studied.

 
Goals:

Finish construction by May 1999 and fly the airplane 100 yards at an elevation of six feet.
