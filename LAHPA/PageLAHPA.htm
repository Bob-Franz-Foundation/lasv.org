Los Altos Human Powered Airplane Prospectus
Project Name: Grasshopper
 
History: The Los Altos Human Powered Airplane project (LAHPA) started after several accomplishments in various competitions. Among these were the 1996 World Solar Challenge in Australia, the 1995 and 1997 Winston Solar Challenge in Dallas, and the 1996 and 1997 Chrysler "Build Your Dream Vehicle" Event. The idea of making the human powered airplane originated with several students. These students felt that after building several successful projects, such as an electric car (Speed Racer 1993), and the two solar cars (Cool Runnings 1994) and (Solar Shadow 1995), the organization should advance to a new level and accomplish what others only dreamed.

The name of the HPA, Grasshopper, was decided among the members of the team. It symbolizes the desire of getting in the air. The LAHPA project was finall initiated during February, 1997.
 
Team Members: Rick Amado, Arthur Chang, Luis Hsu, Danny Huang, Calvin Lee, Alen Lin, Alex Lin, Michael Ni, David Sadamoto, Sam Shin, Jennifer Yang

The work and research were divided among designated members of the team into closely interacting areas. These divisions include computer drafting and designing, Internet researching, and the building of drivetrain, wingspan and other composite components.
 

Description: A human powered aircraft is primarily made of foam, fiberglass, and mylar. It would have an approximate weight of 175 pounds.
 
Research: The book The Gossamer Odyssey by Morton Grosser. Several sites concerning American and Japanese HPA through the World Wide Web was also read and studied.
 
Goals: - finish construction by May 1998.
- fly the human powered airplane 100 yards at an elevation of 6 feet.
