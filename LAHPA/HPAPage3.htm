8/21/99
Nelson is now helping with the LAHPA page. The intended goal this summer was to assemble the entire plane in our school gym, but due to the lack of time we could only get the airframe up. This was still a major goal for us. We now have all our wires labeled and also gained a great deal of experience. Problems were also brought to our attention. The pitch on the prop was incorrect and now we must do some more research to solve that problem. The plan now is to get the plane ready to fly between the months November and December. Another happy note is that Dr. Paul Illian, head of The Raven Project (Raven HomePage), is willing to help us in the flight of the airplane.
10/30/98
Keith is taking over the maintenance of the LAHPA page from Arthur. This is the first update since last year. Over the summer, the members of the LAHPA team went to the San Bernardino International Airport to assemble and fly the plane. After nearly completing the assembly, we attempted a pull-test of the plane when, unfortunately, one of the wing sections broke, damaging an 18 ft. tube. After unsuccessfully trying to fix the plane, we headed back for Los Altos disappointed and very tired. Since the beginning of the school year, we have done several things in a positive direction. First off, we recruited several new team members since we lost the majority of the old team to college. We have held several fund-raisers and have earned several hundred dollars. We have also sent several thank you letters as well as a request to March Airforce Base to use thier hangars to continue constructing the plane as soon as possible. As far as actual construction goes, we have nearly completed the drivetrain/prop assembly and hope to have it spinning by next week. Last, we cleaned up our work room and reorganized everything.
6/21/98
Currently the HPA team is working on assembling the wing section and the propeller. The team has postponed the flight date due to the recent preparation for the S-Class race and some problems in the airport arrangement. However, all members will work throughout summer to finish the plane. The new flight date is now set to be August 1, 1998, and it will be held at San Bernardino International Airport (Norton Airport). Most of the materials have been purchased/donated.
3/16/98
We had a meeting at lunch time and decided to order 28 pieces of 12 feet long, 2 inches wide aluminum tubing. The aluminum tubings cost roughly $2,000! Several questions regarding the connections of the spreaders also came up during the meeting. They are currently being redesigned.
3/14/98
We continued working on the rib templates. Arthur finally updated the LASV.ORG webpage! The 1/16th scale model of Grasshopper is now being worked on. Scaled drawings are also being made.
3/13/98
The rib templates are getting near the final shape. We have decided to purge the usage of balsa for the leading edge. We are now going back to foam which is tougher and cheaper.
3/12/98
The rib templates are continued to be sanded for their original rough cut outs are way larger than the proposed design. Balsa turned out to work well for the leading edge. Balsa is light weight and yet strong enough for our purposes. The price however is a drawback.
3/11/98
Half-rib templates are being made with the full-size rib templates. We have decided to use balsa wood for the leading edge. We tested several pieces of thin balsa (1/8 inch) by soaking them wet with water then bending them into the curved shape of the leading edge. We also had a parent meeting at night. Lots of parents showed up and gave their inputs. It was nice to know that our parents are willing to give us support.
3/10/98
We have finished shaping the full-size rib templates. We came to meet at CSF at night. The wing designs have gone under major redesigning. We have decided to have eight twelve feet wing sections. There will be 4 full size ribs on each wing section instead of 5. The location of the spreaders will be on the third wing section on each side. Half-rib full scale drawings will also be finished soon.
