NOV 12, 1997
We continued gluing trailing edges side by side and edges to the rib support. The frame of the wing is formed. We measured the weight of the wing section. The total weight of the frame is six pounds.

NOV 10, 1997
The trailing edges were connected to the wing.

NOV 6, 1997
The micro bubbles dried up. We also sanded off the excess micro bubbles. The wing is actually pretty stable. Now we are starting to connect the trailing edges to the ribs.

NOV 5, 1997
We continued to glue the leading edges.

NOV 4, 1997
Today we glued the leading edges on to the ribs.

NOV 3, 1997
Today is a clean up day. Everyone was assigned to clean a different part of our workspace.

OCT 31, 1997
The glue dried. Since the support was not the exact size as the edge, the sizes varied. For the ones that are bigger, we decided to sand off the support. We made sure that we did not damage the original trailing edges. The support had the same even surface as trailing edge. For the supports that are smaller than the trailing edge, we decided to use microbubbles to fill up the difference.

OCT 30, 1997
We started hotwiring the supports for the trailing edges. After making the supports, we glued them on to the trailing edges. Since we had to wait for the glue to dry, this is all we did today. The rest of the period we cleaned up our workspace.

OCT 29, 1997
We decided to make supports for the trailing edges. We found the size of each support needed. We also assembled the wing.

OCT 28, 1997
We continued hotwiring the ribs.

OCT 27, 1997
We hotwired the ribs to make them the exact same size as the new templates that we made.

OCT 24, 1997
Today a new alarm system was installed. We could not accomplish what we intended to do because of this.

OCT 23, 1997
We had a meeting this afternoon discussing new plans and goals. We assigned each member different task to accomplish.

OCT 22, 1997
We could not get the table saw to flatten the surface of the trailing edges. We have decided to make new templates. The old trailing edges did not have ending edges as high as the ending edges of the ribs. Thus, we decided to use the new templates to make additional parts for the ending edges of the ribs in order for the old trailing edges to fit.

OCT 21, 1997
We finally decided to use table saw to flatten out the curved surface. If this does not work, we will probably need to make new templates and fix the hotwire in order to make the surface as flat as possible.

OCT 20, 1997
Today is a student release day, but we managed to come in during the morning to work on the wing section. We replaced the wire and made it tighter, hoping that we can make flat surfaced trailing edges. We are still deciding whether to make new trailing edges or to fix the old ones.

OCT 17, 1997
Due to equipment failure and lack of materials, we postponed the deadline for finishing the wing. The hotwire had a defect in which the wire bends. All of the trailing edges did not have a flat surface. We are probably going to replace the wire on the hotwire and start over on the trailing edges.

OCT 16, 1997
We purchased the book Gossamer Odyssey from Barnes & Nobles for each team member in order for each one of us to understand more about human powered airplanes. We also continued working on the trailing edges.

OCT 15, 1997
We continued hotwiring both the trailing and leading edges. We had a meeting this afternoon discussing our accomplishments and our next task. We also discussed about how to get financial support from schools such as CalPoly Pomona and corporations such as Aerovironments.

OCT 14, 1997
We continued hotwiring the trailing edges. The support for the leading edge was also glued on to the front of the ribs using micro bubbles.

OCT 13, 1997
We hotwired the trailing edges for the wing section. We had also started working on the HPA perrspective.

OCT 10, 1997
We used hole saws to cut four 3-inch and four 4-inch holes on the ribs to reduce weight. Every gram reduced means higher efficiency. The weight of each rib before cutting was 130.89 g. After the cutting, each rib weighs about 108 g. Since we had a total of 12 ribs, the total weight saved was 274.68 g.
