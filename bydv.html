Build Your Dream Vehicle

The Los Altos Academy of Engineering has a long-standing history of participating in the Build Your Dream Vehicle Competition. Competing ever since 1996, it has had a legacy of success ever since the competitions conception. The Los Altos Build Your Dream Vehicle teams have accumulated thousands of dollars in prizes for the program and its many projects and, in May 2005, once again brought home the trophy. However, this year was different, the BYDV team didn’t bring home just any trophy--they brought home first.

In October 2004, Mr. Robert Franz, the LAAE advisor, assigned Giselle Obregon, junior, to the task of forming one team that she believed could meet the challenge of the new BYDV assignment to create a car that was “so hot it could make your eyes sweat.” Having one team would mean that it would be a greater concentration of truly talented students since last year, when there were three teams, dispersing the talent and ability, not one made it to the national finals competition. Finally, a coalition of two seniors, Michael Sanora and Leonardo Micolta, five juniors, Richard Westbrook, Helena Xu, Kari Wong, Chris Liu, and Giselle Obregon, and two sophomores Ann Chong and Desiree Magat was formed to be Dynamic Innovations 04-05.

The first task in the competition was to submit a report containting the main components of the competition- model pictures, Finance, Engineering, Ergonomics and Saftey, and Marketing. Out of 320 entries nation wide, Dynamic Innovation was one of the 20 teams that advanced to the semi-finals where each team was required to submit a video with the overall presentaion of the car with presentors. Out of the 20 semi-finals team, Dynamic Innovations was one of the 8 teams that advanced to the finals competition in Detroit, Michigan. Leo Micolta, Giselle Obregon, Kari Wong, Chris Liu, and Helena Xu were among the five students that went to Detroit in May along with Vice Principle Tami Pearson and Drama Department Mr. Fox. Before the national finals, the team practiced and worked together to rehearse the speech, compose the brochure, and finish the model.

The team arrived on May 24th in Detroit where they again prepared for the next day where they would finally be able to present the final completion of the project they had worked all year on. On the last day of the competition, the judges announced the winners of the competition. Out of the 8 final national teams and 320 entries, Dynamic Innovations won 1st place with the grand prize of $6,000.00. The team could not have been so successful if it was not for the support of the parents and benefactors of Engineering. Dynamic Innovations thanks Mitch and Nikki Kodama for their generous competition and help with the presentation. Dynamic Innovations has already begun planning this years competition and hopes to be just as successful as they were last year.
