POWERED BY

05.18.00 - Today the Lightspeed II race team arrived in Topeka, Kansas, where we are staying at the Ramada Inn.  We are planning on waking up bright and early tomorrow morning to get the car ready for scrutineering, and if all goes well, we will be able to test the car on the race track.  The bike team is doing well and will be reassembling their bikes tomorrow from transport and might be able to test ride them.

05.19.00 - This morning we got off to an early start. The bike team finished assembling the two bikes but won't be going through scrutineering until tomorrow morning.  After a long day working on the car, Lightspeed II was finally completed enough to go through scrutineering and passed with flying colors with Jenny driving the car.  Mr. Franz, doubting that the car would be finished and pass through scrutineering, agreed to shave off his moustache as part of a wager he made with the race team.  Tomorrow the Lightspeed II team plans on testing the car on the racetrack and getting Joyce, our second driver, passed through scrutineering.  The bike riders will be warming up tomorrow morning by riding bikes to the racetrack.  After they pass through scrutineering they plan on testing their bikes out on the track.

05.20.00 - The bike teams started the morning with scrutineering but ran into problems with their array.  After adjusting the array to meet the requirements, they passed without any other problems.  In the sprint, Timothy placed second and Tony placed fourth, giving both teams a great starting position.  The race team spent most of the day fine-tuning the car and had the opportunity to run a few practice laps.

05.21.00 - After putting the final touches onto Lightspeed II, Joyce went through the dynamics testing so that she would be qualified as a backup driver.  In the S-Class race, Lightspeed II started off slow and after the first lap had to pit because the hatch came loose.  After fixing the hatch, the car failed to restart and the crew took several minutes to fix the problem.  However, after going just over a quarter mile, the car stopped because of a bad electrical connection.  Another few minutes were spent fixing the problem, but after the car got started again, Jenny smelled something burning in the car and had to stop.  The crew got out to the car and discovered that the power tracker was messed up.  Finally, over an hour after the race started, Jenny was back on the track, turning in times faster than any other team.  In the end, Lightspeed II completed 23 of the 34 laps of the race finishing fifth. 

The bike team had better luck in their race.  Jorge started the race and quickly overtook the lead bicycle and he and Timothy maintained this position through the first half of the race.  Tony and Stewart also did well, ending up tied for third at the break.  Unfortunately, during the charging break, both teams' solar arrays weren't functioning properly and both had to go into the second half of the race without any power.  Jorge stayed strong and maintained his position and Timothy finished the last two laps of the race, giving them a first place finish.  Tony and Stewart, not having any motor assistance, dropped a few positions, but still finished sixth overall.
