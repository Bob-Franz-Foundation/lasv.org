Liability

 

        Liability is frequently regarded as one of the main concerns people have before they become involved in large-scale projects such as the human-powered airplane, Electrathon vehicles, Daimler-Chrysler Build Your Dream Vehicle Competition, and robotics competitions.  We have never had any known injuries in a project.  The liabilities of these projects are fully covered by both the La Puente Valley Regional Occupational Program (ROP) and the Hacienda La Puente Unified School District.  Any sponsors of the project will not be held liable for their participation and support.

 

 

Goal

The goal of this project is an ultra-light hydrogen fuel cell single person vehicle that will travel forty miles per hour without refueling, using six liters of compressed hydrogen. The project will cover the research, design, construction, and demonstration of the vehicle in a one-hour exhibition.

 

Project Evaluation

 

 

        The students dedicate most of their time to this program.  With all the hard work and effort they put into this program, the students really want to meet their goal.  On average 95% of the students attend our meetings every week.  At every meeting we set higher standards for ourselves to gradually work toward the main goal.  During the meeting, the team leaders discuss with the members and set an action list of things that needs to be done and a date that will be done by.  This way it keeps everyone on task so that we won’t fall behind schedule.  It is also a way to see that everyone contributes to the main goal.
