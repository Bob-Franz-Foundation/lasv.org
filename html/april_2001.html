Los Altos Takes First for the Sixth Year in Daimler-Chrysler Competition

By: Jenny Liu

Since the beginning of the Daimler-Chrysler Build Your Dream Vehicle Competition in the Los Angeles region, Los Altos has taken first place every year. This year, the results were no different. Five out of the eight teams competing in the regionals were from Los Altos High School. Each team was given a half hour to present the features as well as the marketing and financial strategies of their dream vehicle Environmental Concepts from Los Altos, with their vehicle, Ascent, took second place overall. Team leader Brian Tagiku along with teammates Bobby Lee, Jessica Lu, Edith Tchon, and Philip Song gave a presentation of their project. James Lee, Daniel Mai, and Joseph Xu focused on displaying the powerpoint presentation, their team's commercial, and the design of their sports sedan. The first place team was Dynamic Innovations with their vehicle, Serenity. Jenny Liu, the team leader along with Alan Wei, Martin Indrajana, Lauren Miyake, and Curtis Wong gave the presentation while Jon Hsiung, Jose Marchen, and George Wu worked on the design aspects of the vehicle. The winning team will fly to Detroit on April 30 to compete in the national competition on May 1.
"I think this competition is fun and exciting and I can't wait to go to Nationals," says team member Jon Hsiung.

American Solar Challenge

By: Grace Chang


The team members of the Los Altos Academy of Engineering are attending the annual American Solar Challenge. This race consists of solar cars from prestigious colleges as well as one other high school. The American Solar Challenge will begin at Chicago, Illinois. Claremont, California will be the location for the Grand Finale for this race. On the 25th of July solar car teams will race into Claremont from Barstow, and will finish on Yale Avenue which is one of the main streets in Claremont. The local business owners and City officials of Claremont have agreed to close Yale Avenue to other traffic so that the solar cars are able to cross the finish line. After passing the finish line, the solar cars will be put on display on the same street. The team members from the Los Altos Academy of Engineering are looking forward to this event and have confidence in there performance. Stay tuned for more further information on this race and the progress of the team.

ZEV 2001

(Zero Emission Vehicles)

By: Anna Wu

The 2nd annual ZEV (Zero Emission Vehicles) 2001 Solar/Electric Challenge will be held at Irwindale Speedway, on Sunday April 29th from 7:00 AM to 3:00 PM. The ZEV 2001 Challenge is a high school event to promote clean air transportation and engineering. It is sponsored by Innovative Schools Advancing Air Quality and the South Coast Air Quality Management District. Scrutineering will be on April 28 from 8:00 AM to 2:00 PM at Los Altos High School. The following high schools will be participating in the race: Alhambra, Bell Gardens, Canyon, Centennial, Diamond Bar, Lakewood, Los Altos, and Redondo Shores. At the ZEV 2001 there will be two different classes. The high school class will last one hour and the electrathon vehicles must have current Electrathon America Membership. The A & B class of solar bikes will last two and a half hours or 100 km. Each team must provide one adult and one student lap counter per vehicle. The entry fee is $20 per vehicle for ISAAQ members and $40 per vehicle for non-member. The general admission is free. There will be a banquet after the meeting for all the participants at Los Altos High School. For more information about the ZEV 2001 or ISAAQ call the Los Altos Academy of Engineering at (626)330-1096.

Progress of Solar Shadow II

By: Hannah Tchon

The students of the Los Altos Academy of Engineering are in the final stages of building Solar Shadow II. Currently, the mechanical team has finished the frame work for the solar vehicle. Now that they have completed their contribution to the solar car, they are working with the composite team to finish the bottom of Solar Shadow II. The composite team is redesigning the solar array so it can be a self-supporting structure. They have completed all the battery boxes for the vehicle. The electrical team has mounted the trackers on a board, and they are cycling all the batteries. Our design team has completed the design for the T-shirts that will be worn to show our LASV pride. Public Relations is currently working on grants for companies such as Coca-Cola Foundation, Boeing, JC Downing, and Honda. They are still in the process of getting sponsors for Adopt-A-Mile. Los Altos Academy of Engineering will be participating in a press conference for television, radio stations, and journalists from Panda TV, Chinese Daily, and KAZN 1300 AM.

Adopt-A-Mile

By: Mirna Zhou

With 1,963 miles to go, the Los Altos Academy of Engineering has raised $3,170 on 317 miles of the 2,300 miles of the race. In an effort to raise money to cover the expenses of going to the race, each student is required to participate by approaching possible sponsors to Adopt-A-Mile. Parents and the community can help support the Academy by adopting a mile of the race, for $10 a mile. By sponsoring $100-$199, a Certificate of Recognition and a LAAE T-shirt will be given. $200-$499, a LAAE T-shirt and a Plaque with Certificate of Recognition. $500 and up, all of the above will be included along with your name on Solar Shadow II.
