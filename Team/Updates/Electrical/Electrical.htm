Electrical Team Updates


Electrical Team Weekly Update
February 11, 2004

Infusion


We have successfully ordered and received our newest battery pack from Worley Energy Cells in Singapore. The lightweight lithium polymer battery pack is a 24V 13.2Ah pack that cost a total of $1750. We have begun drawing schematics to integrate the pack into our fuel cell vehicle system. At the moment, we are building a discharge/charge unit for the unique pack and also purchasing a new 24V variable charger. We have also ordered all the components that we will need to electrically complete Infusion. For rear-view vision, we will use an LCD screen with a small video camera. For the motor/motor controller setup, we have decided to use a system very similar to an electrathon vehicle. We are still waiting on the order for our second fuel cell from Ballard and contemplating the details of our system. Much more research, discussion, and testing is to take place before any of the system is wired.

LightSpeed II/III



The solar arrays for both vehicles have been checked and LSIII's array is still in good shape but LSII is in need of some minor repairs and new connectors to the power tracker. We have checked to make sure that our two Newgen wheel motors and two Newgen motor controllers are all interchangeable with each other in LSIII and that all are in working order. We are awaiting the arrival of several orders for meters, wire, wire shielding, connectors, etc. to finish up the system in LSIII. Our latest problem with LSII is that the frame was built incredibly small and many of the significant electrical components, like batteries, motor controller, and power tracker, either do not fit or fit in a very tight way. So a decision was made to cut off the back end and re-size it so that the motor controller, power tracker, shunts, and fuses will fit under the seat and under the swing arm. Also, instead of using aluminum battery boxes (don't worry, we use insulation), we are now planning on using custom fit composite boxes to create just a little more room for the driver and the battery boxes to fit comfortably. More adjustments are to be made to the frame and electrical system placement. The new meters have been ordered and we are awaiting their arrival as well.
