LS II Chassis


As you may already know, Los Altos Academy of Engineering is rebuilding the LS II Chassis. The new model will consist of extra arm room, new suspension for the front, and a smaller steering box. The frame is now in step two, fabricating. The stock has been cut out, and all there is left to do is assemble it. That is a mighty task, all parts of the frame need to be specially fit in order to come together like a giant puzzle. Once the frame is complete we could start on the suspension, this car is going to have trailing arm suspension. This means that the a-arms would be on the side of the car, going from the front of the car to the wheel location. Next the Mechanical team will hand it over to the Electrical team which will mount all the electronics and batteries to make this car go. Then it’s onto Composites which would fit on the bottom pan, or the under body, to LS II.

Fuel Cell
Torsion Plates – To holding the torsion bars together and provide pick up points for the front suspension.

Bolt Inserts – Made from aluminum, allow the bolts on the bulkheads to sit farther in.

PROBLEM
- Cracks were found in the 4th and 5th bulkheads. Composites are not strong enough to support the weight on the bulkhead.
- Positioning 2nd pick-up point on front suspension without hitting driver’s box.
- Steering box position.
SOLUTION
- 1-inch steel rivets though the 4th and 5th bulkhead will give it the stability it needs.
- Create a mockup of Torsion Plates to find the space limitation.
- Will attach a bar to steering box which will sit under the drivers box, the bar will run and attach to the front bulkhead and connect to a series of gears, this will allow for a second bar to connect to them and run back towards the driver for proper steering capabilities.

LSII
PROBLEMS
- Battery packs do not fit properly in the frame.
- Rear swing arm pick-up points are 2-inches too low
- Inefficient space for electrical components

SOLUTIONS
- Placing an extension on the front of the frame to hold required batteries / Creating composites battery boxes that fit closer to the batteries.
- Remove rear pick-up points and surrounding bars in order to move them higher.
- When moving the rear pick-up point move drivers seat forward 3-inches allowing more space for electrical components.
